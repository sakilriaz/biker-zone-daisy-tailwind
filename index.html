Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

A helmet is considered old and needs replacing if it's more than 5 years old, shows visible signs of wear and tear on the exterior, has a damaged or deteriorated interior padding, has been involved in a crash, or if the manufacturer recommends replacement based on its age or usage history; most safety organizations suggest following the "5-year rule" for helmets regardless of appearance.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before going on a long ride, take your bike around the block and make sure it is safe to ride. First of all, your tires are going to be deflated, it is normal after long periods of time on storage. Inflate them close to the recommended level, you can find that in the wall of the tire. Make sure your brakes work.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

When riding your bike, you should always wear a helmet, obey traffic lights and signs, ride with the flow of traffic, signal your turns, ride on the right side of the road, yield to pedestrians, and be aware of your surroundings, especially when approaching intersections; ensure your bike has working lights for nighttime visibility, and ride in a single file when cycling with a group.
